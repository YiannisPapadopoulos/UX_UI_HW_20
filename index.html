Research & Observations

INTERVIEWS
We conducted 3 remote, 1:1 interviews, or rather a blend of questions & tasks (Usability testings of the current website). We developed this kind of structure because we needed both to identify user navigation pain points & understand user behavior & feelings towards animal adoption.

Key findings
• The user relates pets with the idea of family and home
• The user fails to find photos & details of animals to adopt
• The user thought the site looked ‘under construction’
• The user isn’t encouraged to trust or donate to the website

Home is an important concept applying to everyone. It could be of core value to our project and I want to see if it will be validated in the next steps of our research.

from Research to Design Decisions

The research helped us pinpoint user needs & guided our redesign process. The following list displays the findings that are intrepreted in a flow of wireframes.

  • User needs access to a collection of animal photos.
  • User requires easy access to an adoption application form.
  • Surveys suggested these forms need to be provided in an online format.
  • Almost 75% of the responses belonged to users between the ages of 24 - 44 years old.
  • Website must inspire trust to the user.

The age range of our users drove our decision to elaborate on a mobile-first version of the website.

This wireflow showcases the steps users take to perform the adoption process & illustrates a feature list based on research findings about the user.

    Feature List
  • Clear navigation system
  • Carousel of animal photos
  • Online application form
  • System response / feedback informing user about what will happen (creates trust)

Low-Fi Wireframe Testing

We conducted three, remote one to one usability testings of our low fidelity clicklable prototype.

Key interview findings
The users needed to see proposed donation amounts (i.e. $10, $15). The “Donate” screen was iterated accordingly using dollar amounts on buttons, thus providing this option.

In this frame of mind, we revisited the current site and realized the Paypal option that already existed should be included in the redesign.

This decision provides more options for the user and more channels of funding for the organization.

Furthermore, as a reliable third-party factor, Paypal adds trustworthiness to the organization.

High-Fi A/B Testing

    In this process we tested:
  • Placement of “Donate” button (A.on the header / B. in the hero section)
  • Visibility of the “Adoption” button (A.on the header / B. not visible)
  • Additional “Donate” button in the footer (displayed in version B.)

Key findings
The users preferred clearly the header with both “Donate” and “Adopt” buttons of version A because it becomes immediately obvious that both tasks can be accomplished on the site.

Users also liked the additional “Donate” button in the footer of version B. Both suggestions were incorporated into our final iteration.

Branding

Making a series of meaningful choices in the field of aesthetics was necessary in order to communicate visually a feeling of trust.

LOGO: An important step towards this direction was the logo redesign. The friendly and rounded shapes of cooper black typeface was the initial source of inspiration. The research we made afterwards validated our choice.

The use of cooper black in this artwork was a pleasant surprise, proving a kind of affinity between the typeface and the theme of pets.

COLORS: We wanted our palette to reflect the concept of home. Therefore we designed the interface as a place warm (gold & red) and clean.

Challenges & Learnings

Working in a team is always challenging. This was the first project for our team, which means I had to learn & adjust to the various levels of expertise, availability and engagement to the project of my teammates. Identify the pain points of the group and try to relieve them is the key.

In our research we didn’t manage to reach out the stakeholders despite our efforts. This deprived our project from valuable insight.

Investing more in ideation. This was a weak point in our project that I’d love to elaborate more if I had the chance.

Future Opportunities

  • Design the remaining desktop pages
  • Given our survey findings, designing a testimonials section would be helpful to users.
  • The Carousel is a great way to check out a few photos. But for a large number of animals to be adopted, we should find ways to make searching easier for the user (e.g. create favourite lists, filters etc)
  • The site seems text heavy. We could expand the use of icons (e.g. Facebook, a “+” by “Learn More”), potentially develop customized icons
  • Include a Price and Discount list, which will assist users in making the right financial choices according to their budget
